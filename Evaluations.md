Response B is better than Response A, and it's not particularly close on the engineering. B cascades its deletes, so removing a post takes its comments, likes, and bookmarks with it; A never sets onDelete anywhere, which means Prisma falls back to Restrict and you simply can't delete a user or post that has any children — the database rejects it. B also indexes the feed the way you'd actually query it (publish flag plus date), while A indexes publishedAt alone and leaves the status filter out. On top of that, B caps title, bio, and comment lengths right in the schema where A leaves everything as unbounded String, and B actually says how Zod, DOMPurify, httpOnly cookies, and rate limiting get wired in rather than just npm install-ing them like A does. The honest caveat: neither one actually built the thing — both stop at the design stage — but B is the blueprint you could hand to a team, and A is a setup checklist.
